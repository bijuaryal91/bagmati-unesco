1. ICT CLASSROOM

BAGMATI UNESCO CLUB has successfully completed the ICT CLASSROOM Project in Shree Baljyoti Secondary School. The project was completed in 3 phases including 3 different projects:
I. Digital Library
II. Computer Lab
III. Projection Lab

I. Digital Library:
With the support of InnovateX Pvt. Ltd and online volunteers from Spain and Kyrgyzstan, we successfully provided a digital library to the school in coordination with the Ward Office, Ward 05, Hetauda Sub-Metropolitan City. The library consists of over 2.5 million books from different domains that includes academia, sci-fi, fiction, non-fiction and so on. The best thing about the project is it has been hosted online and everyone from all across the globe have free access to the contents that the web-based library of the school provides. For further information: www.elibrary.baljyoti.net

II. Computer Lab:
The library of Shree Bal jyoti secondary school initially had 3 desktop computers. The school is home to over 500 students and it was really difficult to host over 500 students with 3 desktop computers. Bagmati UNESCO CLUB has provided 4 desktop computers to the school in the first phase of our pilot program to enhance the technical skills of the students. We further plan to add 6 desktop computers in the next 2 months to make it a broader one.

2. Cloud Burst Mapping


Location: Ward 5, Hetauda Sub-Metropolitan City
Project: Blue-Spot Identification
Presentation: Digital Elevation Method identifying the blue spots within the region

Cloudburst is an extreme form of rainfall, sometimes mixes with hail and thunder normally lasting no longer than a few minutes but capable of creating flood conditions. Meteorologists depicts that, the rain from a cloudburst is usually of the shower type, with a fall rate equal to or greater than 100mm (4.94 inches) per hour.Most common, cloudburst occur in connection with thunderstorms. In this storms there are violent uprushes of air, which at times prevent the condensing raindrops from falling to the ground, a large amount of water may thus accumulate at high levels, and if the upward currents are weakened the whole of this water falls at one time. Cloudburst especially occurs in mountainous areas. Cloudbursts occur because the warm air current from the ground or below the clouds rushes up and carries the falling raindrops up with it. The rain fails to fall down in a steady shower. This results in excessive condensation in the clouds as new drops form and old drops are pushed back into it by the updraft. Then one of them gives in. The air current slows down or the clouds can't hold. The resulting violent downpour can dump as much as 70,000 tonnes of water over an acre of land.
