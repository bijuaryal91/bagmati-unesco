Bagmati UNESCO CLUB
Bagmati UNESCO Club.

Bagmati UNESCO Club is a non-governmental organization registered under theOrganizational Registration Act. 2034 B.S. in District Administrative Office, Makawanpur,Nepal. We work under the guidelines of the National Commission for UNESCO in Nepalaccredited by the World Federation of UNESCO Clubs, Association and Centres.

Mission Statement.

As per the UNESCO’s Constitution in Article I, Our mission is to ensure the achievement ofthe vision of UNESCO;

“Contribute to peace and security in the world by promoting collaboration among nationsthrough Education, Science, Culture, and Communication in order to further universalrespect for justice, for the rule of law and for the human rights and fundamental freedomswhich are affirmed for the peoples of the world, without distinction of race, sex, language orreligion”

Aims.

Further the ideals, objectives and programmes of UNESCO and, to that end, further,strengthen and promote international understanding and ​education​, particularly with a viewto the effective exercise of human rights, the endogenous development of every people,and the construction of a new world economic and social order, and pursue these aims alsoin nations and peoples, particularly in order to advance mutual understanding among thedifferent cultures in the world; involve people in UNESCO work, particularly at the grassroots level; promote utilization of science and technology for peaceful purposes, for thewelfare of humanity, and, in particular, of the developing countries; promote in all societiesthe right of every person and every people to a ​culturalidentity​.

Our Goals:

1. To promote the UNESCO ideals, goals and programmes.
2. To strengthen and to develop, together with the WFUCA and the NationalCommission for UNESCO in Nepal
3. To foster the cooperation among the UNESCO Association, Clubs and Centres inNepal through common programs and projects in field such as:
    I. Human Rights, Democracy, Peace and Justice
    II. Education and Culture
    III. Intercultural Dialogue
    IV. Development and Environment
    V. Communication
    VI. Natural and Cultural Heritage (material and environment)
    VII. Science and Technology
